Burton library - Bought through Quaritch, McL., [sic] 1920- Sothebys Sothebys Sothebys Liue with me and be my Loue, And we will all the pleasures proue That hilles and vallies, dales and fields, And all the craggy moutaines yeeld. There will we sit vpon the Rocks, And see the Shepheards feed their flocks, By shallow Riuers, by whose fals Melodious birds sing Madrigals. There will I make thee a bed of Roses, With a tousand fragrant poses, A cap of flowers, and a Kirtle Imbrodered all with leaues of Mirtle. A belt of straw and Yuye buds, With Corall Clasps and Amber studs, And if these pleasures may thee moue, Then liue with me, and be my Loue. Loues answere. If that the World and Loue were young, And truth in euery shepheards toung, These pretty pleasures might me moue, To liue with thee and be thy Loue.
