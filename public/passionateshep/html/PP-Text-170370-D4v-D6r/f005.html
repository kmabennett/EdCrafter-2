Liue with me and be my Loue, And we will all the pleasures proue That hilles and vallies, dales and fields, And all the craggy moutaines yeeld. There will we sit vpon the Rocks, And see the Shepheards feed their flocks, By shallow Riuers, by whose fals Melodious birds sing Madrigals. There will I make thee a bed of Roses, With a tousand fragrant poses, A cap of flowers, and a Kirtle Imbrodered all with leaues of Mirtle.
